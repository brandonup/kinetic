# Problem Statement: Kinetic — AI Consulting Co-Pilot

**Author:** Brandon Upchurch
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Draft

---

## Problem Framing Narrative

**I am:** a consultant launching an AI advisory practice targeting B2B companies under 2,000 employees. I'm pre-launch, building pipeline through networking, meetups, and coffee chats while simultaneously trying to stay current on a rapidly evolving AI landscape. I'm technical but not a developer, managing contacts in spreadsheets and knowledge across scattered notes apps — with no system that ties it all together.

**Trying to:** be the most effective consultant I can be for my clients — deeply understanding their goals, connecting their needs to the right solutions in a fast-moving AI ecosystem, making sound strategic recommendations, and maintaining the kind of responsive, thoughtful relationships that generate trust, referrals, and repeat engagements.

**But:** the work of being a great consultant spans too many disconnected systems. Client context lives in meeting notes. AI trends and research live in bookmarks and articles. Follow-ups live in my head. Frameworks I've used before live in old docs I can't find. Nothing connects a client's pain point to a trend I read about last week to the framework I used with a different client last month. I'm the only integration layer across relationship management, knowledge synthesis, strategic thinking, communication, and time management — and I can't hold it all in my head.

**Because:** existing tools are built for single functions — CRMs track contacts, note apps store text, calendars manage time — but none of them understand the *context* of a consulting practice. No tool synthesizes client goals, domain expertise, relationship history, and strategic frameworks into a unified system that proactively surfaces what matters. And in AI consulting specifically, the landscape changes so fast that static knowledge is a liability.

**Which makes me feel:** like I'm falling behind on every front simultaneously. Warm leads go cold. Knowledge goes unleveraged. Client context gets lost between meetings. I know the information exists to give better advice, ask sharper questions, and build stronger relationships — I just can't access it when it matters. At the pre-launch stage, every gap in my system is a gap in my credibility.

---

## Context & Constraints

- Solo operator, no admin or analyst support — the tool *is* the team
- Technical enough to configure AI tools but not coding daily — the system must work without deep maintenance
- Portland, OR market — heavy in-person networking (meetups, events, coffee chats)
- Pre-revenue — must be low-cost to operate and fast to deliver value
- AI landscape changes weekly — captured knowledge needs recency signals, not just volume
- Client-specific context is sensitive — each client's data should be isolated (folder-per-client model)
- Building for personal use first — product-market fit for other consultants is a future consideration, not a current constraint

---

## Final Problem Statement

A solo AI consultant needs a way to operate with the depth and responsiveness of a full consulting team — synthesizing client context, domain knowledge, relationship history, and strategic frameworks into actionable intelligence that helps solve client problems — because existing tools silo these functions into disconnected apps, forcing the consultant to be the manual integration layer and causing client insights to go unleveraged, advice quality to suffer, and early-stage credibility to erode.
